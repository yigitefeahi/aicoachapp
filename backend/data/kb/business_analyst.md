# Business Analyst Interview Knowledge Base

Business Analyst interviews evaluate requirements gathering, stakeholder communication, process analysis, KPI definition, documentation, reporting, prioritization, and translating business needs into clear product or system requirements.

Strong CV signals include user stories, acceptance criteria, BPMN or process maps, Jira, Confluence, SQL reporting, Excel, Power BI, Tableau, stakeholder workshops, gap analysis, UAT, and measurable operational improvements.

Good answers explain the business problem, stakeholders, constraints, current process, proposed requirement or process change, tradeoffs, validation method, and business outcome. Strong candidates show both analytical thinking and communication with technical/non-technical teams.

Common prompts include: gather requirements for a new dashboard, handle conflicting stakeholder priorities, improve a manual workflow, define acceptance criteria, analyze a process bottleneck, or validate whether a solution met business goals.
