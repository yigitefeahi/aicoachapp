# Data Analyst Interview Knowledge Base

Data Analyst interviews evaluate SQL, Excel, dashboarding, business metrics, data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and the ability to translate ambiguous business questions into measurable insights.

Strong candidates mention tools such as SQL, Excel, Power BI, Tableau, Looker, Python, pandas, or statistics, but the strongest evidence is business impact: dashboards built, KPIs defined, reporting automated, trends explained, cohorts analyzed, or decisions influenced.

Good answers explain the business question, data source, cleaning/validation steps, metric definition, analysis method, insight, recommendation, and measurable outcome. They should avoid only listing tools without explaining decisions or impact.

Common prompts include: define a KPI for a product funnel, investigate a drop in conversion, clean inconsistent data, build an executive dashboard, explain a trend to non-technical stakeholders, or compare customer segments.
