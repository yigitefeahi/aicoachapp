# Interview Answer Frameworks

Strong interview answers usually combine a clear structure, a concrete decision, and measurable impact. For behavioral prompts, use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result. The action section should show personal ownership, tradeoffs, and communication. The result section should include a metric, business outcome, learning, or follow-up action.

For technical prompts, start with assumptions and constraints before describing the solution. Explain the architecture or debugging path step by step, name the tradeoff, and close with validation through tests, monitoring, rollout, or user impact.

For system design and product cases, clarify goals first. Define success metrics, key users, constraints, core components, risks, and alternatives. A strong answer ends with a recommendation and one thing to measure after launch.

Common anti-patterns include vague team-only answers, missing metrics, no discussion of alternatives, jumping to implementation before constraints, and ending without a result or validation plan.
