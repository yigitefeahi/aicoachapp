# Evidence Backed Interview Prep Drills

Story compression drill: choose one project story and rewrite it into a 90-second answer. Keep one sentence for context, two or three sentences for actions, and one sentence for measurable result. Success means the answer has personal ownership, a metric, and a clear lesson.

Technical depth drill: take one past technical decision and explain constraints, alternatives, selected approach, validation, and tradeoff. Success means an interviewer can understand both the engineering choice and why it mattered.

Company bar drill: pick a target company rubric and rewrite an answer to emphasize its strongest signals. For impact-heavy companies, add metrics and ownership. For quality-heavy companies, add edge cases, customer impact, and long-term maintenance.

Case sprint drill: spend five minutes clarifying goals and metrics, ten minutes structuring options, and five minutes making a recommendation. Success means the answer includes assumptions, success metric, tradeoff, and next experiment.

Tone cleanup drill: record an answer, remove hedging, shorten long sentences, and repeat it. Success means fewer filler words, clearer verbs, and a stronger closing result.
