# Evaluation Rubric And Faithfulness Signals

High-quality feedback should cite the candidate answer, role expectations, and retrieved evidence. It should not invent facts. When retrieval quality is low, the evaluator should rely on the answer and generic rubric rather than forcing weak evidence.

Technical depth is high when the candidate explains constraints, architecture, tradeoffs, implementation details, validation, and operational impact. Communication is high when the answer is structured, concise, and specific. Problem solving is high when alternatives and decision criteria are explicit.

Faithful RAG feedback connects each major suggestion to a relevant source: role expectations, company rubric, answer framework, CV signal, user memory, or graph relation. Low-confidence feedback should warn that evidence coverage is limited.

Common scoring risk signals are too-short answers, off-topic answers, vague language, missing metrics, missing ownership, and lack of validation.
