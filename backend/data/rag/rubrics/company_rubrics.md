# Company Interview Rubric Signals

Google-style loops reward structured problem solving, technical depth, data-informed tradeoffs, and clear handling of ambiguity. Good answers state assumptions, compare alternatives, and explain why the chosen path fits scale and user needs.

Meta-style loops reward impact, ownership, execution speed, metrics, and crisp communication. Good answers show how the candidate moved quickly, measured outcomes, and influenced product or engineering direction.

Stripe-style loops reward precision, user empathy, long-term thinking, operational quality, and clear writing. Good answers explain customer impact, edge cases, maintainability, and principled tradeoffs.

Amazon-style loops reward ownership, customer obsession, bias for action, measurable results, and operational rigor. Good answers show personal responsibility, hard decisions, and outcomes backed by numbers.

Apple-style loops reward craft, simplicity, quality, privacy, and cross-functional influence. Good answers show attention to detail, strong taste, and careful product judgment.
