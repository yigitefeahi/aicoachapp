# Interview Answer Anti-Patterns

Weak answers often describe what the team did without explaining the candidate's own actions. They also skip measurable outcomes, avoid tradeoffs, and end without validation or learning.

Technical anti-patterns include jumping into tools before constraints, naming architecture buzzwords without reasoning, ignoring failure modes, and not explaining how the solution was tested or monitored.

Behavioral anti-patterns include vague conflict stories, blaming others, missing reflection, and not making the result clear. Strong coaching should convert these into specific next steps.

Case anti-patterns include not clarifying the goal, using a framework mechanically, ignoring user segments, and making a recommendation without metrics or risks.
